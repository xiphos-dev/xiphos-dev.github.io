StarCraft II strategy recognition

Computer Science thesis. I designed multiple data representations for in game observations to be translated into input vectors for dense, recurrent and convolutional neural networks. Each case was then tested to find the best candidate. The goal being to ascertain the feasibility of using these networks to solve the task of strategy recognition, in particular, strategies used by human players. This problem is relevant since it pertains to the manifold challenge of creating an artificial agent capable of playing complex strategy games.
