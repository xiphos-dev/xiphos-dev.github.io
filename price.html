Price2Be

Our team developed an assistance tool for online marketplace vendors. The user must input some basic information about its product, such as brand name, model and category, e.g., electronics, kitchen utensils or sports clothing. Price2Be will then output relevant data about the product, such as popular tags that can be used to market the item on social media, the item's popularity measured by search queries related to it broken down by region inside Chile, among other pieces of useful information to vendors. Using a dense neural network, our tool will also provide a price suggestion for the item, while also showing the price projection for the same item for the 3 upcoming months. Some products are seasonal, and thus their prices are influenced by the time of year, while others remain fixed. The price projection can give insight about the shifting prices associated to a particular item.

Designing a solution for price suggestion

  • Parse raw sales data and employ feature engineering.
  • Train and test dense neural networks.
  • Pipeline deployment to allow price suggestion to be integrated with other modules.

Pictured above are the error metrics obtained for a given category subgroup in the dataset, a model was trained for each one. Since the networks used were doing a regression over a logarithmically scaled value for the price variable, the error metric chosen was the Root Median Squared Logarithmic Error (RMSLE). In this case, a value closer to zero indicates better perfomance. Although, as a proof of concept, a single model was used for all predictions, later analysis led to training different models for particular categories. This was decided due to the difference in quantity of training data for each case and the detrimental effect on performance brought by the lack of training examples. Consequently the final version of Price2Be used 16 models for price prediction, thus insulating the better performing product categories from the ones that had less data available, while preserving minimally expected performance on every use case.
